## Key Differences in Translation Philosophy

### 1. Localization vs Authenticity

**Yen Press:**
- Removes cultural markers (honorifics, loanwords)
- Converts measurements and references
- Targets broader Western audience
- Professional editorial polish

**MTL Studio:**
- Preserves cultural markers (-san, -kun, senpai, onii-chan)
- Keeps original measurements
- Targets light novel enthusiasts
- Assumes reader familiarity with Japanese culture

**Example:**
- Yen: "senior colleague (a real babe)"
- MTL: "beautiful senpai at work"

---

### 2. Internal Thought Formatting

**Yen Press:**
- Uses "I felt like saying..." / "I thought..."
- Integrates thoughts into narrative prose
- More subtle distinction between narration and thought

**MTL Studio:**
- Uses *italics* for direct internal thoughts
- Clearer visual distinction
- More immediate, less filtered

**Example:**
- Yen: "I felt like saying to her, 'Uh, excuse me...'"
- MTL: "*Um, miss. Your own outfit is pretty tacky...*"
